Hokkaido
Hokkaido is a captivating region known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, delicious cuisine, and year-round outdoor activities. It offers pristine wilderness, beautiful national parks, vibrant seasons, fresh seafood, and a blend of indigenous Ainu heritage and modern Japanese influences.
Tohoku
Tohoku offers a compelling blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Travelers to this region can immerse themselves in breathtaking landscapes, discover hidden historical gems, savor delicious cuisine, and witness vibrant festivals.
  • Akita
  • Aomori
  • Fukushima
  • Iwate
  • Miyagi
  • Yamagata
Kanto
Kanto is a vibrant region that includes Tokyo and its surrounding prefectures. It offers a mix of modernity, traditional culture, and historical landmarks. Visitors can explore Tokyo's bustling streets, ancient temples and shrines, historic neighborhoods, and enjoy culinary delights ranging from sushi to street food.
  • Chiba
  • Gunma
  • Ibaraki
  • Kanagawa
  • Saitama
  • Oita
Chubu
Chubu is a region known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and gastronomic delights. It offers the majestic Japanese Alps, iconic Mount Fuji, historic cities , rejuvenating hot springs, and a variety of delicious cuisine including fresh seafood and soba noodles.
  • Aichi
  • Fukui
  • Gifu
  • Ishikawa
  • Nagano
  • Niigata
  • Shizuoka
  • Toyama
  • Yamanashi
Kansai
Kansai is a culturally rich region renowned for its historical significance, ancient traditions, and delicious cuisine. It features traditional charm of Kyoto, the energetic street food culture and modern cityscape of Osaka, the friendly deer and well-preserved neighborhoods of Nara, and the cosmopolitan flair and famous Kobe beef of Kobe.
  • Hyogo
  • Kyoto
  • Mie
  • Nara
  • Osaka
  • Wakayama
Chugoku
Chugoku offers a blend of history, natural beauty, and culinary delights. From the historical significance of Hiroshima to the tranquil beauty of Okayama's gardens and the unique landscapes of Tottori, Chugoku provides a captivating experience for travelers. Whether you're interested in history, nature, or gastronomy, Chugoku has something to offer every visitor.
  • Hiroshima
  • Okayama
  • Shimane
  • Tottori
  • Yamaguchi
Shikoku
Shikoku is a charming and serene region located in southwestern Japan, known for its tranquil landscapes, spiritual sites, and cultural traditions. As the smallest of Japan's main islands, Shikoku offers a peaceful retreat away from the bustling cities.
  • Ehime
  • Kagawa
  • Kochi
  • Tokushima
Kyushu
Kyushu is a captivating region in southwestern Japan, known for its diverse landscapes, vibrant cities, hot springs, and cultural heritage. Whether it's vibrant urban centers, serene hot springs, or cultural landmarks, Kyushu offers a diverse and captivating experience for travelers.
  • Fukuoka
  • Kumamoto
  • Kagoshima
  • Nagasaki
  • Miyazaki
  • Saga
  • Oita
Okinawa
Okinawa is a tropical paradise within Japan, offering beautiful beaches, vibrant marine life, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. Whether you're seeking relaxation on sandy shores, exploring underwater wonders, or immersing yourself in local traditions, Okinawa provides a unique and unforgettable experience.
